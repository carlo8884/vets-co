Vetster earns our top pick by being the only telehealth platform that consistently connects pet owners with licensed, practicing veterinarians rather than technicians or advisors. Every vet on Vetster is licensed in their state, and you can view their credentials, experience, and specialties before booking — the same vetting process you'd apply to an in-person vet visit.

The video consultation format is the right model for telehealth — it allows the vet to observe your pet's gait, breathing, skin condition, and behavior in real time, which significantly improves the quality of guidance compared to chat-only platforms. Our test consultations consistently produced specific, actionable clinical guidance rather than generic advice.

At $50–75 per consultation, Vetster is priced as a premium service — but given you're getting a real licensed vet on a video call rather than a chat with an advisor, the comparison is to an after-hours vet visit, not to a monthly subscription app.

AskVet's 5-minute average response time is genuinely impressive and solves the most common use case for pet telehealth — the 11pm "is this something I should be worried about?" question that doesn't warrant an emergency vet visit but also can't wait until morning. The unlimited questions subscription model means you're not rationing questions by cost.

The team consists of licensed veterinarians, though consultations are chat-based rather than video. For behavioral questions, diet guidance, and "should I be concerned" triage, the format works well. For anything requiring visual assessment — skin conditions, wound evaluation, gait analysis — video is the right medium and Vetster is the better call.

At $30/month for unlimited access, AskVet pays for itself after one or two middle-of-the-night questions that would otherwise have meant an emergency vet bill or an anxious night waiting for the clinic to open.

If you already buy from Chewy, their Connect with a Vet service — included free with Chewy+ ($19.99/month) — is genuinely compelling. The platform integrates directly with your Chewy account, which means prescription orders flow directly into your existing cart and delivery schedule without switching platforms. For chronic medication management (thyroid meds, allergy medications, joint supplements) this convenience is meaningful.

The quality of veterinary consultations is comparable to Vetster — licensed DVMs, video capability, 24/7 access. The value proposition depends entirely on whether the Chewy+ membership pays for itself through the other benefits (free 1-2 day shipping, discounts on prescriptions). For high-prescription households, it often does.

💰 Most Affordable · Best for Budget
PetCoach by PetSmart
Chat-Based · Certified Coaches + DVMs · $8.25/month
7.8
Expert Score

PetCoach offers the lowest price point in the category at $8.25/month, but comes with an important caveat: many responses come from certified pet coaches rather than licensed veterinarians. For behavioral training questions, basic nutrition guidance, and general care questions, certified coaches provide competent answers. For any health-related concern, you need a DVM — and on PetCoach, routing to a vet is not guaranteed.

As a supplemental resource for a highly engaged pet owner who already has a regular vet relationship and uses the service primarily for behavioral and training guidance, it delivers value. As a replacement for veterinary consultation, it doesn't.
