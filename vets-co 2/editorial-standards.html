Our Editorial Standards

Every piece of health content on Vets.co is written by credentialed professionals and reviewed by licensed veterinarians before publication. This page explains exactly how that process works — and what it means for you.

Our Core Commitment

Vets.co exists to help pet owners make better decisions about their animals' health. That mission only works if the information we publish is accurate, current, and grounded in veterinary science rather than commercial interest.

We maintain a strict separation between editorial content and commercial relationships. Advertisers and affiliate partners have no influence over our editorial decisions, our rankings, or our recommendations. A product does not appear in our recommendations because a brand paid us — it appears because our expert team determined it was the best option for our readers.

Our Editorial Principle

We publish only what we would tell a close friend who asked us to recommend a vet, explain a diagnosis, or evaluate a pet insurance plan. If we wouldn't stake our personal credibility on it, we don't publish it.

How Our Content Is Created

1
Topic Selection Based on Genuine Need
Our editorial team identifies topics based on actual questions pet owners are asking — search data, reader submissions, and questions raised in clinical settings by our veterinary advisors. We do not create content to serve commercial interests or rank for profitable keywords at the expense of accuracy.
2
Written or Developed by Credentialed Professionals
Health and clinical content is authored or co-authored by licensed veterinarians, board-certified specialists, or certified veterinary technicians. Non-clinical content (product reviews, insurance comparisons) is developed by our editorial team with mandatory expert review before publication.
3
Veterinary Review Before Publication
Every health-related article is reviewed by at least one licensed veterinarian with relevant clinical experience before it goes live. The reviewing veterinarian's name and credentials are listed on the article. This is not a formality — reviewers have full authority to require changes or block publication.
4
Regular Review and Updates
Veterinary medicine evolves. We review all clinical content at least annually, and immediately when significant new evidence or guidelines emerge. The date of the most recent review is shown on every article. Content that cannot be verified as current is either updated or removed.
5
Corrections Policy
If we publish inaccurate information, we correct it promptly and transparently. Corrections are noted on the article with the date and nature of the correction. We do not quietly edit errors — we document them. To report an error, contact editorial@vets.co.

How We Handle Commercial Relationships

Affiliate links: Some links on Vets.co are affiliate links — if you purchase through them, we receive a commission at no extra cost to you. Affiliate relationships do not influence our editorial recommendations. We link to products we recommend; we do not recommend products because we have affiliate relationships with them.

Advertising: Vets.co displays advertising. Advertisers are clearly identified. No advertiser has editorial influence over our content, rankings, or vet listings. Sponsored content, where it exists, is clearly labeled as such.

Vet listings: Veterinarians listed in our directory are not ranked by payment. Our directory is organized by relevance to the user's search, distance, specialty match, and verified credential status. Vets cannot pay for higher placement in organic search results.

Medical Disclaimer

Content on Vets.co is intended for informational purposes only. It is not a substitute for professional veterinary advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always seek the guidance of a licensed veterinarian for questions regarding your pet's health condition. Never disregard professional veterinary advice or delay seeking it because of something you have read on Vets.co.

In an emergency, contact your nearest emergency veterinary clinic immediately. Do not rely on website content to make emergency medical decisions.
